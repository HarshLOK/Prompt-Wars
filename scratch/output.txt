Executive Summary
The Smart Venue Experience System (SVES) is an integrated digital platform designed to transform the experience of attending large-scale sporting events. By combining mobile applications, IoT sensor networks, AI-driven crowd analytics, and centralized staff coordination tools, the system addresses the three most critical pain points in physical events: crowd bottlenecks, excessive waiting times, and fragmented communication.
The platform serves three distinct user groups — Attendees who need real-time guidance and services, Staff who require operational dashboards and coordination tools, and Venue Operators who demand strategic analytics and infrastructure integration. The expected outcome is a measurable reduction in crowd-related incidents, shorter average wait times by 40%, and a significant uplift in attendee satisfaction scores.
1. Product Goals & Objectives
1.1 Vision Statement
To make every large-scale sporting venue a seamlessly managed, data-driven environment where attendees move confidently, staff operate proactively, and venue operators continuously improve through real-time intelligence.
1.2 Business Goals
Reduce crowd-related safety incidents by 60% within the first season of deployment.
Decrease average gate entry time from 8 minutes to under 3 minutes.
Achieve a Net Promoter Score (NPS) of 70+ for the attendee mobile app within 6 months.
Increase revenue per attendee by 20% through in-app pre-ordering and targeted notifications.
Enable venue operators to cut staffing inefficiency by 25% through intelligent task routing.
Establish the platform as a scalable SaaS product deployable across multiple venues globally.
1.3 Product Objectives
Build a real-time crowd density monitoring system with sub-2-minute data refresh.
Launch a cross-platform mobile app (iOS and Android) for attendees.
Deliver a browser-based operations dashboard for staff and venue managers.
Integrate with existing venue systems: ticketing, CCTV, POS, and transport APIs.
Implement AI-based predictive congestion alerts with at least 85% accuracy.
Support accessibility features for elderly, disabled, and non-English speaking visitors.
1.4 Scope
In Scope

4. Feature Requirements
Features are organized by user group and tagged with priority. All High-priority features are required for the Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
  ATTENDEE-FACING FEATURES
  F-ATT-01  |  Digital Ticket & QR Entry
Attendees receive a unique QR code upon booking. The code is stored in the app and functions offline. Gate scanners read the code in under 1 second and update the entry system in real time.
Offline QR code storage (no network required at scan)
Dynamic QR refresh every 30 seconds to prevent screenshot fraud
Integration with venue ticketing API for real-time validation
Accessibility: code enlargeable for elderly users
  F-ATT-02  |  Live Interactive Venue Map
A fully navigable map of the venue including seating zones, food courts, restrooms, first aid, exits, and accessible routes. Updated in real time with crowd density overlays.
Indoor navigation with step-by-step directions to any venue point
Color-coded crowd density indicators (green / amber / red)
Accessible route highlighting for mobility-impaired users
Search functionality: 'nearest restroom', 'Section B entrance'
  F-ATT-03  |  Real-Time Queue & Wait Time Display
Live wait-time estimates for all entry gates, food stalls, merchandise counters, and restrooms, derived from sensor data and updated every 60 seconds.
Gate-by-gate estimated wait time in minutes
Smart recommendation: 'Gate 7 is 4 mins faster right now'
Historical trends shown for planning (e.g. half-time rush prediction)
SMS fallback for users without smartphone data connection
  F-ATT-04  |  Food & Merchandise Pre-Ordering
Attendees can browse menus, place orders, and pay in-app with a timed pickup slot. Orders are routed to the nearest or least-busy service point.
In-app menu with real-time item availability
Payment via UPI, card, and wallet integrations
Timed pickup slots to reduce counter crowding
Order tracking with ready-to-collect notification
  F-ATT-05  |  Push Alerts & Notifications
Context-aware, personalized notifications delivered via push, SMS, and in-app banner. Covers gate changes, match updates, safety alerts, and service promotions.
Priority tiering: Emergency > Operational > Informational > Promotional
Location-based alerts (e.g. 'Your nearest food stall is now ready')
Do-not-disturb setting with emergency override
Broadcast capability for venue-wide emergency messages
  F-ATT-06  |  Accessibility Support
Dedicated accessibility mode providing wheelchair-accessible route guidance, large text options, hearing loop zone indicators, and priority service information.
Accessibility toggle in onboarding and settings
Dedicated accessible entrance navigation
Companion ticket linking and group navigation
Screen reader compatibility (WCAG 2.1 AA compliance)
  STAFF-FACING FEATURES
  F-STF-01  |  Crowd Monitoring Dashboard
A real-time web-based dashboard providing a venue-wide overview of crowd distribution, queue lengths, and service utilization. Accessible on tablet and desktop.
Live heatmap updated every 60 seconds from IoT and camera feeds
Zone-by-zone crowd count and capacity percentage
Queue length indicators for all monitored service points
Alert panel with color-coded urgency levels
  F-STF-02  |  Task Assignment & Team Coordination
A structured task management module allowing supervisors to create, assign, and track tasks in real time. Team members receive tasks on their mobile devices.
Task creation with zone, type, priority, and assignee fields
In-app acknowledgement and status updates from field staff
Shift scheduling integration with task auto-routing
Audit trail of all task assignments and completions
  F-STF-03  |  Incident Reporting System
A structured form for logging crowd incidents, medical events, security concerns, or infrastructure issues. Reports are automatically routed to the relevant department.
Incident type taxonomy with mandatory fields
Photo and voice note attachment capability
Auto-escalation based on incident severity
Integration with venue emergency response protocols
  F-STF-04  |  Communication & Announcement Panel
A centralized interface for sending announcements to attendees via app notification, digital signage, SMS broadcast, and PA system integration.
Pre-written announcement templates for common scenarios
Zone-targeted or venue-wide broadcast selection
Message scheduling for pre-planned announcements
Delivery confirmation log with read receipts where available
  VENUE OPERATOR FEATURES
  F-VEN-01  |  Operational Command Centre
A comprehensive venue-level dashboard aggregating all operational data streams — crowd, services, staff, incidents, and revenue — into a unified command view.
Multi-zone overview with drill-down capability
Live KPI cards: gate throughput, average wait time, incident count, revenue
Configurable alert thresholds with escalation rules
Role-based access control for different management levels
  F-VEN-02  |  AI Congestion Prediction Engine
Machine learning models trained on historical event data and real-time sensor feeds to predict congestion hotspots up to 15 minutes in advance.
Zone-level congestion probability scores updated every 5 minutes
Event-type calibration (final match vs. regular season)
Automated routing suggestions pushed to attendee app
Model accuracy dashboard for continuous improvement
  F-VEN-03  |  System Integration Hub
API-driven integration layer connecting SVES with existing venue infrastructure: ticketing systems, CCTV platforms, POS terminals, and transport management.
RESTful API with OAuth 2.0 authentication
Webhook support for real-time data push from ticketing providers
POS data ingestion for concession demand analytics
Transport API integration for shuttle and parking coordination
  F-VEN-04  |  Post-Event Analytics & Reporting
Automated post-event reports delivered within 4 hours of event close, covering crowd flow, wait times, revenue, incidents, and staff utilization.
Standardized PDF report with executive summary and charts
Interactive data explorer for drill-down analysis
Benchmarking against previous events of similar type and size
Export to CSV, Excel, and BI tool connectors (Tableau, Power BI)
  F-VEN-05  |  Emergency Response Coordination
A dedicated emergency mode activatable by authorized operators, triggering coordinated responses across all channels simultaneously.
One-click emergency activation with scenario presets
Automated crowd direction overlay on attendee maps
Staff mobilization with role-specific instructions
External agency notification (police, medical) with location data

6. Technical Architecture Overview
6.1 Technology Stack
Mobile App: React Native (iOS & Android), offline-first with local QR caching
Staff Dashboard: React.js web application, responsive for tablet and desktop
Backend: Node.js microservices with GraphQL API gateway
AI/ML Engine: Python (TensorFlow/PyTorch) for crowd prediction models
Database: PostgreSQL for transactional data, Redis for real-time caching, ClickHouse for analytics
IoT Data Pipeline: Apache Kafka for sensor stream processing
Cloud Infrastructure: AWS (ECS for containers, RDS, ElastiCache, S3, CloudFront)
Notifications: Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) + AWS SNS for SMS
Authentication: OAuth 2.0 / JWT, Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)
6.2 Data Sources
IoT sensors: Bluetooth/Wi-Fi probe data for crowd density estimation
CCTV analytics: Computer vision API for people counting and queue detection
Turnstile and gate scanners: Entry/exit counts per gate per minute
Ticketing system: Pre-event attendance projections by zone
POS terminals: Real-time sales data by concession zone
Mobile app: GPS and beacon-based indoor positioning
6.3 Security & Compliance
All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
DPDP Act 2023 (India) compliant data collection and consent flows
QR codes use HMAC-SHA256 signing with rolling secrets
CCTV data processed locally with anonymized crowd counts sent to cloud
Role-based access with MFA required for staff and operator accounts
Annual third-party security audit and penetration testing

8.1 Assumptions
Venue has existing Wi-Fi infrastructure capable of supporting concurrent attendee connections
At least 65% of target attendees own a smartphone with iOS 14+ or Android 10+
Venue operator will provide API access to existing ticketing, POS, and CCTV systems
Staff will be trained on the dashboard tools before deployment
IoT sensor hardware procurement and installation is managed separately by venue operations team
8.2 Constraints
System must function with degraded sensor data (minimum 70% sensor uptime) without complete failure
App must work in offline / low-connectivity mode for core features (QR code, saved map)
Budget ceiling for v1.0 development: to be confirmed by product leadership
Launch deadline aligned with start of next domestic cricket / football season
All data storage must be India-resident for compliance with DPDP Act 2023

01  —  Design Philosophy
Core Aesthetic Direction
Every screen in the SVES platform should feel like a mission control environment — dense with real data, zero decorative clutter. The design language is built on four principles:
DARK OPS COMMAND
Operators trust the system because it looks like it means business. No decorative elements. Pure information architecture.
GOLD AS SIGNAL
Gold marks live values, active states, and primary actions only. When a number glows gold, it is happening right now.
PRECISION GRID
Sharp 2px corners. 1px borders. Pixel-level alignment. The grid is visible in the discipline of the layout itself.
DATA DENSITY
Mini-charts, sparklines, and delta indicators live alongside primary numbers. Staff can read status in a 2-second glance.

07  —  Design Rules
Do / Don't
DO
DON'T
+ Use Arial Bold for all numeric data — it reads at a distance
- Never use rounded pill corners — sharp 2px defines precision identity
+ Reserve gold exclusively for live and primary interactive elements
- Never use gold for decoration — it loses meaning as a live-data signal
+ Show delta indicators (±%) next to every changing metric card
- Never show more than 4 metric cards per horizontal row
+ Use 2px left-border on cards to visually prioritize alert panels
- Never use bright white backgrounds — breaks the dark environment
+ Label all axis ticks in Courier New at 10–11pt
- Never animate during an active emergency incident
+ Display timestamps in 24h format with seconds for operations
- Never mix font sizes below 10pt — readability is non-negotiable
+ Maintain 4px base grid — all spacing must be a multiple of 4
- Never stack multiple critical alerts without visual hierarchy between them
+ Use ShadingType.CLEAR for all table and card fills
- Never use percentage widths in tables — always DXA for compatibility

Touch targets 44px+. Map-centric full-viewport layout. Bottom sheet overlays for wait times and navigation. Color coding: green = clear, gold = moderate, red = busy. Simplified metric display — no sparklines. Font minimum 14pt for outdoor visibility.

Inclusive Design Requirements
Contrast ratio
All text on dark surfaces must meet WCAG 2.1 AA minimum — 4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for large text
Minimum font
10pt absolute minimum. Operational text for staff: 12pt+. Attendee app: 14pt+
Touch targets
44px minimum height and width for all interactive elements on mobile
Focus states
2px gold outline on all keyboard-focusable elements. No focus outline suppression
Screen readers
All data tables include proper headers. Charts include text fallback descriptions
Color alone
Never use color as the only differentiator. Alert levels include text label + dot + border color
Motion
Provide reduced-motion mode that disables all non-essential animations
Emergency alerts
Must be conveyed via audio (venue PA integration) in addition to visual display

Attendee app: dedicated toggle enabling large text, accessible routes, companion linking, hearing loop zones
